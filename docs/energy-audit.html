Energy Audits

⚡ Energy Audits: Illuminating Stewardship

Goal: To identify where our buildings are “leaking” energy and money, allowing us to redirect those resources toward mission and ministry.

🔌 Why Audit Your Energy?

The “greenest” kilowatt-hour is the one you never use. Most older church facilities can reduce their energy bills by 20–30% simply by identifying air leaks and optimizing aging HVAC systems.

🛑 1. The Simple DIY Audit

Before spending money on a professional, your “Green Team” can identify the “low-hanging fruit” in a single afternoon.

  • The Draft Test: On a windy day, use a lit incense stick or a thin tissue near windows, doors, and electrical outlets. If the smoke or tissue flutters, you have an air leak that needs weatherstripping or caulk.
  • Lighting Inventory: Count every bulb in the building. If it isn’t an LED, mark it for replacement. Switching to LEDs is the single fastest ROI in energy stewardship.
  • The “Phantom Load” Search: Identify electronics that stay plugged in 24/7 (coffee makers, old CRT televisions, printers). Use power strips to truly turn them off when not in use.
  • Thermostat Audit: Ensure thermostats are not “fighting” each other and are programmed to drift (higher in summer, lower in winter) during hours the building is unoccupied.

⚠️ 2. Utility-Sponsored Audits

Many local power providers offer free or low-cost basic assessments for commercial and non-profit customers.

  • CenterPoint Energy (Houston): Through their SCORE Program (Schools and Conserving Resources), CenterPoint provides energy performance benchmarking and technical assistance to help non-profits identify efficiency projects.
  • Austin Energy / CPS Energy: These utilities often provide free on-site energy walk-throughs and significant rebates for attic insulation and HVAC upgrades.
  • Why do this? Utility audits are excellent for securing rebates. They often pay for a portion of the hardware if you follow their specific efficiency recommendations.

✨ 3. Professional Diocesan Audits

The taskforce is currently running a few pilot audits with a well-regarded professional company that specializes in church audits. It is hoped that we can work out a deal for the diocese, which may include financing for congregations that are under-resourced. More to come in this space.

A comprehensive audit generally runs about $5,000.

What a Professional Audit Includes:

  • Thermal Imaging: Using infrared cameras to see exactly where heat is entering or escaping through walls and ceilings.
  • HVAC Life-Cycle Analysis: A detailed report on when your units will likely fail and what high-efficiency models should replace them.
  • Project Prioritization: A data-backed list of projects ranked by their “Payback Period” (how long it takes for energy savings to pay for the upgrade).
